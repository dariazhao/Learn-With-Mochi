The world we see

A generation is growing up inside sealed machines.

Today's devices are miracles of engineering, and perfectly sealed black boxes. Unlike the clunky Commodore 64 that invited tinkering, modern technology masks its own inner workings. When technology feels like magic, children learn to consume it. When it feels like a language, they learn to create with it.

We believe computational thinking is a foundational literacy, as essential as reading, writing, and arithmetic. It should begin not at age 12 in a computer lab, but at age 3 on a living room floor, with wooden blocks and a curious bear.

Technology is too important to be limited to the 0.01% creating it in Silicon Valley.
Where we come from

Two immigrants. One question. A bear.

Mark

Post-Soviet Ukraine. A grandmother who taught him nothing was ever broken, just waiting for imagination. Princeton, Oxford, Forbes 30 Under 30, and 100K computer kits shipped. Then a bigger question: what about the youngest children?

Daria

From China to Canada, barely speaking English. A refugee family took her in and taught her generosity can exist even when you have so little. Wall Street analyst turned STEM teacher. She traded her salary for free office space and a bigger mission.

The Build

Every VC said no. "Make it an app." But a screen-free product delivered through screen time made no sense. So they laser-cut blocks from Home Depot plywood, recorded audio on Daria's phone, and tested in friends' living rooms.

Launch

Christmas 2019. Funded in under 12 hours. 720 backers. $218K raised, over 4x their goal. Every single kit arrived on time.

What we stand for

Four convictions that shape everything we build.

Maria Montessori proved over a century ago that children construct knowledge through their senses. Neuroscience has confirmed it: motor engagement during learning strengthens memory formation. Our coding blocks are natural wood. Our adventure maps are fabric you unfold on the floor. Our rover moves through real space. None of this is nostalgic. It is neurological.
Every Mochi adventure is built around narrative: a curious bear, a new world to explore, a problem to solve. The coding concepts (sequences, loops, functions, decomposition) are embedded in the story, not bolted on. A child doesn't "learn to code." They help Mochi travel the solar system, and the code is how they get there.
Mark arrived from Ukraine. Daria from China. They know what it means to be outside a system that wasn't designed for you. Learn with Mochi is a public-benefit corporation because the mission isn't to capture a market. It's to expand who gets to participate in the technological future. We've shipped to 62 countries across six continents. The product arrives as a blank slate so every child can create in their own image.
Not metaphorically. Literally. Children form hypotheses, test them, observe outcomes, and revise their understanding, naturally, instinctively, joyfully. Our system is designed for open-ended play: no single "right answer," no score, no fail state. Only what the child imagines, codes, and discovers for themselves.

Screen-free learning tools designed from the ground up for child safety, developmental science, and play.

Why this matters

Children deserve technology designed with their development in mind.

Most technology built for children optimizes for engagement metrics, not developmental outcomes. As a public-benefit corporation, Mochi is legally structured to prioritize child wellbeing over growth. When the interests of business and the interests of children conflict, our charter requires us to choose children.

Our principles

Five pillars that guide everything we build.

Research consistently links early excessive screen exposure to delayed speech, reduced attention, and limited social engagement. Mochi is physical, tactile, and multi-sensory. Our wooden blocks and fabric maps meet children where developmental science says they learn best: through their hands.
Montessori's insights about self-directed learning and tactile exploration have been validated by modern neuroscience. Motor engagement during learning strengthens memory. Haptic feedback improves spatial reasoning. Every Mochi component is a neurological tool informed by over a century of research.
Children construct meaning through narrative. Every Mochi adventure has a story arc: a character, a journey, a problem to solve. Coding concepts like sequences, loops, and functions are embedded in the story. A child does not "do a coding exercise." They help Mochi explore the solar system, and the code is how they get there.
There is no score in Mochi. No timer. No wrong answer. When children are freed from the anxiety of evaluation, they experiment more boldly, persist longer, and develop deeper understanding. Play is not the opposite of learning. It is the mechanism of it.
Mochi arrives as a blank slate. No gendered marketing, no cultural assumptions, no language barriers. Our kits ship to 62 countries across six continents because computational thinking is a universal literacy, not a privilege. Every design decision is tested against one question: does this work for every child?
